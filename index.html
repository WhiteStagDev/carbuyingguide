  • 2WD ‚Äì Cheapest, best for cities & flat roads
  • AWD ‚Äì Good for rain, ice, light snow
  • 4WD ‚Äì Best for off-road, snow, mud, or towing heavy stuff

Do you need All-wheel drive (AWD), Two wheel drive (2WD), or Four wheel drive (4WD)?

The biggest difference is that a 2WD transmission will send power to two wheels, while 4WD uses all four wheels. 2WD vehicles are excellent at driving on dry, flat roads and they can be more affordable than 4WD and AWD vehicles, which often carry a premium price tag and tend to be slightly less fuel efficient.


However, AWD vehicles are great for rain, light snow, or icy roads, as it automatically distributes power to the wheels that need it the most, improving stability and control.


4WD vehicles are great for off-roading and tough, challenging driving conditions such as snow, mud, sand, and steep inclines. They are also used for carrying heavy loads and towing.

Do I want a 4 cylinder engine or a 6 cylinder engine?

1. 4-Cylinder Engine

Generally, 4-cylinder engines are smaller and produce less horsepower compared to 6-cylinder engines. They are typically found in compact cars, sedans, and economy vehicles. These engines are more efficient but may struggle to deliver the same level of acceleration and performance as a 6-cylinder engine.


A 4-cylinder engine offers better fuel efficiency because it burns less fuel. These engines can sometimes be rougher, especially when they are under heavy load or when accelerating quickly. Smaller engines tend to vibrate more, which can result in a less smooth driving experience.


Vehicles with 4-cylinder engines are usually more affordable, both in terms of purchase price and maintenance costs. These engines are less complex, so repairs and parts tend to be cheaper.


Due to their lower power output, 4-cylinder engines are typically less suited for towing heavy loads. While they can handle light towing tasks, they may struggle with larger trailers or heavier cargo.


In terms of driving experience, 4-cylinder engines tend to be more efficient for everyday driving, offering sufficient power for most city and highway needs. However, some drivers may find them less exciting.


2. 6-Cylinder Engine

With two more cylinders, 6-cylinder engines produce more power and torque. This means better acceleration, higher towing capacity, and improved performance at higher speeds. You'll typically find 6-cylinder engines in mid-sized to larger vehicles, such as trucks, SUVs, and higher-performance sedans.


A 6-cylinder engine generally uses more fuel because it has more cylinders. A 6-cylinder engine offers smoother performance, as the power is distributed more evenly across more cylinders. This reduces vibration and provides a more refined driving experience, especially at higher speeds or under load.


Vehicles with 6-cylinder engines are often more expensive, both in terms of the price and maintenance. Repairs or replacements may cost more.


A 6-cylinder engine, especially a V6, generally provides better towing capacity. It‚Äôs a better choice for people who need to tow boats, trailers, or carry heavy loads.


In Summary:

  • 4-Cylinder Engines: Offer better fuel efficiency, lower cost, and are often used in compact cars. They are ideal for those looking for affordability and efficiency, but might lack the power for heavy towing or fast driving.
  • 6-Cylinder Engines: Provide more power, better acceleration, and smoother performance, especially for larger vehicles or those used for towing. They use more fuel, cost more to maintain, and can be heavier. But they go faster and offer a smoother ride.

üíµ Step 10. How Much Car Payment Can I Afford?

13% of your take-home pay (after taxes) for just the car payment seems to fall within a reasonable range. However, keep in mind that the total cost of owning and operating the vehicle will be higher when you add things like insurance and maintenance. Make sure that your remaining income can cover other essential living expenses, such as rent/mortgage, utilities, food, medical, savings, and other spending.


The size of the car payment will depend on the car price, loan term (months to pay it off), interest rate, and down payment. If you opt for a longer loan term (ex. 72 or 84 months), your monthly payment might be more affordable, but the total cost of the car over time, will be higher.


The most common loan term for car purchases in the U.S. typically ranges from 60 months (5 years) to 72 months (6 years). However, the most popular term tends to be 72 months. According to data from various automotive industry sources, about 40% of car buyers opt for a 72-month loan, especially as car prices have risen, and monthly payments need to be spread over a longer period to make them more affordable.

üìà Step 11. What Does "Interest Rate" Mean?

Interest rate is the cost that you pay to the lender (bank) for borrowing money to buy your car (car loan). Interest is the extra money you pay when you borrow money. It's like an extra charge for using someone‚Äôs money. If you borrow $10 from a friend, they might ask for $1 back or 10% as a thank-you for letting you use their money. That extra $1 is interest. The bigger the loan, the more interest you might have to pay.

üìä Step 12. What is a Credit Score?

It all depends on your credit score. A credit score is a number that shows how good you are at paying back money you owe. It's like a report card for your borrowing habits. The higher your score, the better you are at managing debt. Lenders, like banks, use your credit score to decide if they should give you a loan and how much interest they might charge you.


Credit scores usually range from 300 to 850. A higher score means you're seen as a low‚Äërisk borrower, while a lower score might mean you're a higher risk. Check your credit for free in under two minutes at creditkarma.com.

If everything checks out, then go look at the car. Make sure that you test drive the car. Expect to spend a few hours at the dealership. It takes a long time to secure a loan and do all of the paperwork.

Should I Get Pre-Approved Before I Go to the Dealership?

A pre-approval for a car loan is an agreement from a lender (like a bank, credit union, or online lender) that indicates they are willing to lend you a certain amount of money to buy a car, based on a preliminary review of your financial situation.


Key points about pre-approval: They will perform a credit check, and give you information about the potential loan amount, interest rate, and loan term (how long you‚Äôll take to repay the loan). A pre-approval is typically valid for a limited time (e.g., 30-45 days), so you‚Äôll need to finalize the purchase within that period.
